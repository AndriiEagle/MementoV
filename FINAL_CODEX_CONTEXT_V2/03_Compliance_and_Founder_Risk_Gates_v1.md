# Compliance and Founder Risk Gates v1

Purpose: keep future Codex sessions from building strategy on top of founder-level legal-financial assumptions that may be wrong, unstable, or outside the codebase.

This file is not legal advice. It is a project-risk filter.

## 1. Why This File Exists

The additional audit material surfaced a recurring danger:

- product decisions,
- founder survival decisions,
- grant hopes,
- welfare/status assumptions,
- and legal/media assumptions

were too easy to blend into one story.

That is dangerous.

The product can be well designed and still be launched on top of bad founder assumptions.

## 2. Hard Risk Gates

Future Codex sessions must not silently assume any of the following are solved:

### A. `Verein` as a magic firewall

Do not assume:

- a `Verein` automatically protects the founder;
- a `Verein` automatically neutralizes income classification;
- a `Verein` automatically makes grant handling simple;
- a `Verein` automatically solves welfare or employment conflicts.

Treat `Verein` as a separate legal-operational decision, not a product fact.

### B. Grant money as "safe by default"

Do not assume:

- support money is automatically neutral;
- every grant is clearly non-income;
- grant structure is irrelevant to social aid or founder obligations.

Treat grant inflows as classification-sensitive.

### C. `Status S` / welfare / AWA formalities as minor

Do not assume:

- they are "just paperwork";
- monetization can start first and be explained later;
- project structure can ignore employment or reporting consequences.

Treat these as real gates, not side paperwork.

### D. Pro Helvetia as a core strategy pillar

Do not assume:

- Pro Helvetia is the main launch path;
- eligibility is certain;
- funding timing matches product timing.

Treat it as optional upside, not foundational runway.

### E. Real-person imagery and realistic Zurich media as easy

Do not assume:

- real persons in AI imagery are safe without consent;
- logos, trademarks, branded transit, or recognizable protected surfaces are fine by default;
- "realistic Zurich" means you can freely imitate every visible asset.

Default safer mode:

- original environments;
- inspired-by language;
- exteriors-only assumptions where appropriate;
- zero real-person AI imagery without clear consent;
- zero deepfake-style positioning.

## 3. Product-Safe Operating Mode

The safest build mode is:

- web-first MVP;
- no immediate dependency on app-store monetization;
- no dependency on grants for the first useful product;
- no dependency on unresolved founder payout structure;
- no dependency on risky IP/media shortcuts;
- no dependency on emotionally risky "after death" features.

## 4. Separate the Layers

Keep these layers separate:

### Product layer

- UX
- AI flows
- copy
- trust
- safety
- architecture

### Founder-risk layer

- personal residency/status obligations
- social aid implications
- grant classification
- reporting and employment formalities
- payout timing

### Market layer

- family buyer demand
- elderly usability
- Swiss trust
- channel strategy

If these layers blur together, the project becomes harder to reason about and easier to sabotage accidentally.

## 5. Questions That Must Be Answered Before Aggressive Monetization

Before turning on serious revenue paths, someone must answer:

- who receives money first: founder, entity, or platform account?
- what reporting obligations are triggered?
- what consent and sharing rights apply to legacy data?
- what deletion and heir-rights rules must be honored?
- what is the safe product claim boundary for wellbeing, memory, and legacy?

If these remain unclear, keep monetization narrow and reversible.

## 6. Questions That Must Be Answered Before Grant-Led Strategy

Before making grants central:

- is eligibility real and current?
- is the entity structure compatible?
- does the project narrative fit the actual program scope?
- would acceptance create obligations that distort the product?
- what happens if the grant does not arrive?

If the product collapses without a grant, the strategy is weak.

## 7. Questions That Must Be Answered Before Media Expansion

Before adding richer visuals or public marketing assets:

- are all visual assets original or licensed?
- are there any real people represented?
- are there recognizable protected brands or locations?
- is there any deepfake ambiguity?
- is the "inspired-by" line still safe and honest?

## 8. What Codex Should Do When These Gates Are Unclear

If founder-risk or compliance facts are unclear, Codex should:

- not guess;
- not present legal assumptions as settled;
- design the product so it can survive under stricter assumptions;
- keep cashflow architecture, payouts, and public claims conservative;
- say explicitly which decision needs human legal or operational verification.

## 9. Red Flags Requiring a Stop-and-Verify

Stop and verify if any of these appear:

- "this should probably be fine legally";
- "we can clean up consent later";
- "we will explain the money flow after launch";
- "the `Verein` probably covers this";
- "realistic Zurich is fair enough";
- "a dead-relative or after-death mode would be great later".

## 10. Final Constraint

The product should be built so that it remains valuable even under the stricter version of reality:

- slower monetization;
- narrower claims;
- tighter consent;
- tighter founder constraints;
- tighter Swiss trust expectations.

That is the safer and stronger design path.
